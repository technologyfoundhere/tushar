Hey! I’m Tushar - a biomedical engineering (and budding computer vision and robotics) researcher with a big curiosity about how neural networks and medical imaging can actually make a difference in healthcare. My past research is all about using deep learning and neural networks to help doctors spot diseases faster and more accurately, whether it’s monkeypox, dengue, or different kinds of cancer. I love exploring the abstract side of things too: What does it mean for an algorithm to “see” like a doctor? How can we make AI not just smart, but also understandable and trustworthy? More recently, I picked Carnegie Mellon University (along with being one of the department's fellowship recipients) to pursue my foray into graduate studies due to its reputation as the world's best robotics school to transition from medical AI to surgical robotics that makes use of med-AI!

Outside the lab, I’m a huge comics fan (Marvel and DC—yes, I actually read the comics, not just the movies and TV shows, though I do love a good superhero flick or series when it’s done right!). Music is a big part of my life too; I play the piano and keyboard, and there’s nothing like getting lost in movie scores—playing them or just listening! You’ll probably find me out on a bicycle expedition, chasing new trails! Until 2023, I was also an active blogger at TechnologyFoundHere (currently inactive but it'll be back soon) where I used to write about everything that interested me (the blog long outgrew its name, but I couldn't change the catchy branding).

One thing I really believe in is community-based learning. I’m always happy to help out junior students with their projects or coursework, and I’m constantly inspired by the advice and mentorship I get from my seniors and professors. Serving as the secretary, then chairperson and research head of my undergrad school's chapter of IEEE EMBS was particularly rewarding as I realized that for me, research isn’t just about papers and experiments—it’s about sharing ideas, growing together, and having some fun along the way! If you want to talk science, comics, music, or anything in between, feel free to reach out!
